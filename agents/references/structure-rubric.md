# Structure Rubric

## 1. Homepage Priority

- Is the most important information visible early?
- Does the homepage establish identity, research focus, and useful next steps?

## 2. Above-the-Fold Efficiency

- Does the first screen communicate enough without overload?
- Is there obvious wasted space or oversized elements?

## 3. Navigation

- Is the nav small, clear, and sufficient?
- Are page names obvious and unsurprising?

## 4. Section Order

- Are sections arranged in the order a visitor would naturally expect?
- Does the About page feel logical at first glance?

## 5. Page Roles

- Does each page have a clear purpose?
- Is there structural duplication between pages?

## 6. Scanability

- Are headings, groupings, and lists easy to skim?
- Can a visitor find the major categories of information quickly?

## 7. Simplicity

- Is the structure as simple as it can be without losing important content?
- Are there too many sections, wrappers, or conceptual layers?

## Reviewer Language

Prefer comments like:

- "the homepage gives too much weight to the hero and not enough to the next step"
- "the About page hierarchy is confusing on first scan"
- "this section belongs lower/higher on the page"
- "the navigation is correct, but the page itself still feels structurally busy"
