# Content Rubric

## 1. Specificity

- Does the text say concrete things instead of generic academic phrases?
- Are research interests, teaching areas, and contributions described clearly?

## 2. Clarity

- Can a first-time visitor quickly understand who Eric Quintane is?
- Is the prose easy to scan without becoming choppy?

## 3. Substance

- Does each page provide enough useful information?
- Are there areas where the text feels thin or merely decorative?

## 4. Distinct Page Purpose

- Does each page do a different job?
- Is content repeated unnecessarily across Home, About, and Teaching?

## 5. Credibility

- Are claims proportionate and supportable?
- Does the text sound professional and grounded rather than promotional?

## 6. Concision

- Is there any filler, repetition, or inflated phrasing?
- Could the same point be made more cleanly?

## 7. Tone

- Is the tone calm, intelligent, and direct?
- Does it sound like a serious scholar site rather than a marketing page?

## Reviewer Language

Prefer comments like:

- "this paragraph is generic and could be more specific"
- "the page repeats information already stated on the homepage"
- "the visitor still does not learn enough about teaching/research here"
- "this sentence sounds promotional rather than informative"
