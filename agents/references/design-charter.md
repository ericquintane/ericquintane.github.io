# Design Charter

These are hard constraints for future design rounds. Agents should treat them
as boundaries, not suggestions.

## Approved Baseline

The approved baseline is the restrained white version of the site that the
human described as "much better" before the failed experiment.

Future design proposals must clearly beat that baseline on the narrow target
being tested.

## Hard Constraints

- White background only.
- One font family only.
- No beige, cream, or warm-tinted page background.
- No gradients, shadows, or decorative effects unless explicitly requested.
- No increase in overall visual complexity.
- No new page types or navigation items without approval.
- No large homepage hero that dominates the first screen.
- No dead whitespace created by secondary modules inside the homepage hero.
- No proliferation of heading scales.
- No new uppercase micro-label systems without approval.
- Do not crop portrait or profile images in a way that cuts off the head or face.

## Current Priority Problems

Only optimize these unless the human expands the scope:

1. Homepage hero scale and above-the-fold efficiency
2. Typography consistency on the About page

## Typography Rules

- One family across the whole site
- One body size system
- One page-title scale
- One section-heading scale
- One smaller card/subsection-heading scale

If a proposal adds more typographic variation than it removes, reject it.

## Change Control Rules

- Make the smallest change that solves the target problem.
- Do not "freshen up" unrelated areas in the same round.
- Preserve the approved baseline everywhere outside the current target area.

## Human Authority

The human is the final decision maker.

Agents propose.
Reviewers critique.
Coordinator filters.
Human decides.
