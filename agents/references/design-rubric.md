# Design Rubric

Score each area mentally from weak to strong.

## 1. Modernity

- Does the site feel current without looking trendy?
- Is the typography contemporary and intentional?
- Does the visual system avoid dated academic-web conventions?

## 2. Consistency

- Do typography, spacing, borders, and link treatments feel like one system?
- Are similar elements styled similarly across pages?
- Is the homepage aligned with the tone of the interior pages?

## 3. Hierarchy

- Is it obvious what to read first, second, and third?
- Do headings feel proportional to the content?
- Does the visual weight match the importance of the section?

## 4. Restraint

- Is there unnecessary decoration?
- Are there too many boxes, pills, shadows, or accent treatments?
- Does the design stay quiet enough for the content to lead?

## 5. Professional Fit

- Does the site feel right for a management scholar?
- Is it polished without looking corporate or sales-oriented?
- Does the design communicate credibility?

## 6. Spatial Efficiency

- Is above-the-fold space used well?
- Is there enough breathing room without obvious waste?
- Does the homepage hero fit the scale of the rest of the site?

## 7. Mobile Quality

- Does the design remain clear and calm on narrow screens?
- Do line lengths, spacing, and type size stay reasonable?

## Reviewer Language

Prefer comments like:

- "visual hierarchy is too top-heavy"
- "type scale is inconsistent between hero and section headings"
- "the page feels too sparse/dense above the fold"
- "link treatment is cleaner than button treatment here"
