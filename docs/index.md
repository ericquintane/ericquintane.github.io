    <p class="section-kicker">About</p>
    <h2>Research with a structural lens on organizations</h2>
    <p>
      I study the dynamics of interpersonal networks and their consequences for
      individuals and organizations. My work sits at the intersection of
      organizational behavior, social networks, and social capital, with a
      particular interest in how relationships shape performance, learning, and
      well-being.
    </p>
    <p>
      Alongside my role at ESMT Berlin, I serve as an Honorary Research Fellow
      in the School of Psychological Sciences at the University of Melbourne.
      My recent TEDxESMTBerlin talk explores how workplace relationships can
      quietly contribute to exhaustion and burnout.
    </p>
    <a class="text-link-arrow" href="/about/">Read more about my background</a>
  </section>

      <h3>Gender, Network Recall, and Structural Holes</h3>
      <p>
        Examines how network structure shapes recall accuracy and why women’s
        advantage in recalling workplace ties weakens in sparse networks with
        structural holes.
      </p>
      <a class="text-link-arrow" href="https://doi.org/10.1111/peps.12691" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Read publication</a>
    </article>
    <article class="feature-card">
      <p class="feature-card__meta">2023 • Organization Science</p>
      <h3>The Strain of Spanning Structural Holes</h3>
      <p>
        Shows that brokerage can become emotionally draining, increasing both
        burnout and the risk of abusive behavior toward coworkers.
      </p>
      <a class="text-link-arrow" href="https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2023.1664" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Read publication</a>
    </article>

      <p class="feature-card__meta">2022 • Academy of Management Journal</p>
      <h3>When People Build Networks That Hurt Their Performance</h3>
      <p>
        Separates the networks people prefer to build from the networks in
        which they perform best, highlighting unintended consequences of
        person-network fit.
      </p>
