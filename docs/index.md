## Bio

I study the dynamics of interpersonal networks and their consequences for
individuals and organizations. My work sits at the intersection of
organizational behavior, social networks, and social capital, with a particular
interest in how relationships shape performance, learning, and well-being. I
hold a PhD in Management from the University of Melbourne and also serve as an
Honorary Research Fellow in the School of Psychological Sciences at the
University of Melbourne. My recent TEDxESMTBerlin talk, <a href="https://www.ted.com/talks/eric_quintane_4_relationship_traps_that_lead_to_burnout" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">4 relationship traps that lead to burnout</a>, explores how workplace relationships can quietly contribute to exhaustion.
