## 2025

### Gender, Network Recall, and Structural Holes
**Publication:** *Personnel Psychology*

**Coauthors:** [Matthew E. Brashears](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Matthew+E.+Brashears), [Helena V. González-Gómez](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Helena+V.+Gonzalez-Gomez), [Raina A. Brands](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Raina+A.+Brands)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1111/peps.12691)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper studies whether men and women differ in their ability to recall
organizational networks accurately. It shows that women's recall advantage is
stronger in dense and cohesive networks, but weakens in sparse structures with
structural holes where triadic closure assumptions become less reliable.

</details>

## 2023

### The Strain of Spanning Structural Holes: How Brokering Leads to Burnout and Abusive Behavior
**Publication:** *Organization Science*

**Coauthors:** [Jung Won Lee](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Jung+Won+Lee), [Sun Young Lee](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Sun+Young+Lee), [Camila Umaña Ruiz](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Camila+Umana+Ruiz), [Martín Kilduff](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Martin+Kilduff)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2023.1664)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper examines the psychological downside of brokerage. It argues and
shows that keeping people apart as a broker can become emotionally draining,
increasing burnout and even abusive behavior toward coworkers.

</details>

## 2022

### When People Build Networks That Hurt Their Performance: Structural Holes, Cognitive Style, and the Unintended Consequences of Person–Network Fit
**Publication:** *Academy of Management Journal*

**Coauthors:** [Gianluca Carnabuci](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Gianluca+Carnabuci)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.5465/amj.2021.1227)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper separates the network structures people create from the network
structures in which they perform best. It shows that cognitive style shapes
both network formation and performance, and that those two processes do not
always align in beneficial ways.

</details>

## 2021

### Temporal Brokering: A Measure of Brokerage as a Behavioral Process
**Publication:** *Organizational Research Methods*

**Coauthors:** [Martin Wood](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Martin+Wood), [John Dunn](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=John+Dunn), [Lucia Falzon](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Lucia+Falzon)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1177/10944281211002911)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This article develops a behavioral measure of brokerage based on sequences of
time-stamped interactions. Instead of inferring brokerage from position alone,
it captures the extent to which actors actually arbitrate between others or
bring them together in practice.

</details>

### Protecting their turf: When and why supervisors undermine employee boundary spanning
**Publication:** *Journal of Applied Psychology*

**Coauthors:** [Julija Mell](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Julija+Mell), [Giles Hirst](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Giles+Hirst), [Andrew Bruce Carnegie](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Andrew+Bruce+Carnegie)

**Links:** [Repository version](https://pure.eur.nl/en/publications/06ffb108-a78d-4f39-ac10-6b1b8f2ebd65), [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1037/apl0000960)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper shows that boundary spanning can trigger territorial reactions from
supervisors. When leaders interpret employees' external information seeking as
undermining their control over a domain, they are more likely to respond by
undermining those employees.

</details>

## 2020

### The Power, Accuracy, and Precision of the Relational Event Model
**Publication:** *Organizational Research Methods*

**Coauthors:** [Aaron Schecter](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Aaron+Schecter)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1177/1094428120963830)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This methods paper evaluates when relational event models perform well and when

## 2019

**Publication:** *Victims & Offenders*

**Coauthors:** [Mónica Pérez-Trujillo](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Monica+Perez-Trujillo), [Ana L. Jaramillo-Sierra](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Ana+L.+Jaramillo-Sierra)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper studies the social environment of potential victims rather than only
individual or perpetrator characteristics. It identifies connectedness to

</details>

## 2018

### Embedding time in positions: Temporal measures of centrality for social network analysis
**Publication:** *Social Networks*

**Coauthors:** [Lucia Falzon](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Lucia+Falzon), [John Dunn](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=John+Dunn), [Garry Robins](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Garry+Robins)

**Links:** [Repository version](http://hdl.handle.net/1992/47043), [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.socnet.2018.02.002)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper introduces temporal versions of centrality measures that account for
when paths and interactions occur, not only whether they exist. The result is a
set of position measures that better capture influence and accessibility in
time-sensitive interaction systems.

</details>

<details>

This article proposes that digital communication traces can help identify early

risk.

### Investigating the Temporal Dynamics of Interorganizational Exchange: Patient Transfers among Italian Hospitals
**Publication:** *American Journal of Sociology*

**Coauthors:** [James A. Kitts](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=James+A.+Kitts), [Alessandro Lomi](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Alessandro+Lomi), [Daniele Mascia](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Daniele+Mascia), [Francesca Pallotti](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Francesca+Pallotti)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper studies patient transfers among Italian hospitals as a dynamic
exchange system. It finds that reciprocation is shaped by both immediate
resource dependence and longer-term embedding in past exchange relationships,
with each mechanism operating on a different time horizon.

</details>

### How Do Brokers Broker? Tertius Gaudens, Tertius Iungens, and the Temporality of Structural Holes
**Publication:** *Organization Science*

**Coauthors:** [Gianluca Carnabuci](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Gianluca+Carnabuci)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1287/orsc.2016.1091)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper moves from brokerage positions to brokerage behavior. It shows that
brokers use distinct temporal strategies, shifting between keeping others apart
and bringing them together depending on whether they are acting through
established ties or more short-term, unembedded interactions.

</details>

### The microstructures of network recall: How social networks are encoded and represented in human memory
**Publication:** *Social Networks*

**Coauthors:** [Matthew E. Brashears](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Matthew+E.+Brashears)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.socnet.2014.11.003)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This study examines how people store and retrieve network information from
memory. The evidence suggests that people tend to encode networks through
triads or small groups rather than isolated dyads, and that the preferred
representation changes with the structure of the network itself.

</details>

### Heard it through the grapevine: Indirect networks and employee creativity
**Publication:** *Journal of Applied Psychology*

**Coauthors:** [Giles Hirst](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Giles+Hirst), [Daan van Knippenberg](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Daan+van+Knippenberg), [Jing Zhou](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Jing+Zhou), [Cherrìe Jiuhua Zhu](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Cherrie+Jiuhua+Zhu)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1037/a0038333)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper argues that creativity depends not only on direct contacts but also
on the structure of the indirect network that those contacts open up. Access to
nonredundant second-hand contacts increases exposure to diverse ideas and can
support higher creativity under the right conditions.

</details>

## 2013

### Short- and long-term stability in organizational networks: Temporal structures of project teams
**Publication:** *Social Networks*

**Coauthors:** [Philippa Pattison](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Philippa+Pattison), [Garry Robins](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Garry+Robins), [Joeri Mol](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Joeri+Mol)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.socnet.2013.07.001)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper studies project-team communication networks across multiple time
horizons rather than treating them as static snapshots. It shows that different
network structures display different kinds of stability over short and long
periods, which matters for how researchers interpret team dynamics.

</details>

## 2011

### Matter Over Mind? E-mail Data and the Measurement of Social Networks
**Publication:** *Connections, 31(1)*

**Coauthors:** [Adam M. Kleinbaum](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Adam+M.+Kleinbaum)

**Links:** [PDF](http://faculty.tuck.dartmouth.edu/images/uploads/faculty/adam-kleinbaum/mind_over_matter.pdf)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This paper compares e-mail trace data with survey-based network measures inside
organizations. It shows that the two approaches capture meaningfully different
patterns and that survey respondents tend to overstate ties to high-status
contacts while understating ties to people who are physically or
organizationally distant.

</details>

### Innovation as a knowledge-based outcome
**Publication:** *Journal of Knowledge Management*

**Coauthors:** [R. Mitch Casselman](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=R.+Mitch+Casselman), [B. Sebastian Reiche](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=B.+Sebastian+Reiche), [Petra A. Nylund](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Petra+A.+Nylund)

**Links:** [Official article](https://doi.org/10.1108/13673271111179299)

<details>
<summary>Abstract summary</summary>

This article reviews competing definitions of innovation and separates
process-based from outcome-based views. It argues that knowledge management
research benefits from a clearer understanding of innovation as a knowledge
based organizational outcome rather than only as a process.

</details>
