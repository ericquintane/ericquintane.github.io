Eric Quintane

Researching how networks shape work, leadership, and well-being.

I am an Associate Professor of Organizational Behavior at ESMT Berlin. My research examines how interpersonal networks influence outcomes such as creativity, collaboration, and burnout.

Bio

I study the dynamics of interpersonal networks and their consequences for individuals and organizations. My work sits at the intersection of organizational behavior, social networks, and social capital, with a particular interest in how relationships shape performance, learning, and well-being. I hold a PhD in Management from the University of Melbourne and also serve as an Honorary Research Fellow in the School of Psychological Sciences at the University of Melbourne. My recent TEDxESMTBerlin talk, The social side of burnout, explores how workplace relationships can quietly contribute to exhaustion.

Research

Networks, organizational behavior, organizational design, and social capital.

Teaching

Organizational behavior, organizational theory, social networks theory and methods, and change leadership.
